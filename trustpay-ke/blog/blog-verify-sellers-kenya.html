How to Verify Online Sellers in Kenya

Complete guide to verifying sellers before making payments. Spot fake profiles and protect your money.

With the explosion of online marketplaces in Kenya, verifying sellers has become a critical skill. Whether you're buying from Jiji, Facebook Marketplace, Instagram, or WhatsApp, knowing how to verify a seller can mean the difference between a successful purchase and losing your money to a scammer.

Why Verification Matters

Scammers create convincing profiles that look legitimate. They use stock photos, fake reviews, and even stolen content from real businesses. Without proper verification, you have no way of knowing who you're really dealing with.

Essential Verification Steps

1. Check Online Presence

  • Social media accounts: Check if they have active, established profiles
  • Look for consistency: Same name, photos, phone number across platforms
  • Check engagement: Real businesses have comments, replies, and interactions
  • Beware of new accounts: Recently created profiles with no history

2. Verify Phone Numbers

  • Search the number: Google the phone number to see if it's associated with scams
  • Check WhatsApp: See when they were last seen and their profile picture
  • Call the number: A legitimate seller will answer questions
  • Verify area code: Kenyan numbers should start with +254 or 07XX

3. Request Documentation

  • Business registration: Ask for business registration documents
  • ID verification: Request to see their national ID (blur sensitive info)
  • Proof of address: Utility bill or bank statement
  • Product sources: Ask where they source their products

4. Analyze Communication

  • Grammar and spelling: Professional sellers communicate clearly
  • Response time: Legitimate businesses respond promptly
  • Attitude: Scammers often use high-pressure tactics
  • Willingness to answer questions: Real sellers are happy to provide information

5. Check Reviews and References

  • Request references: Ask for previous customers
  • Verify reviews: Check if reviews seem genuine
  • Search for complaints: Google the seller name with "scam" or "fraud"
  • Join community groups: Ask for seller reputation in relevant Facebook groups

Red Flags to Watch For

Major Warning Signs

  • Only communicates via WhatsApp or SMS
  • Refuses to meet in person or video call
  • Price too good to be true
  • Uses stock photos only
  • Pressures you to pay quickly
  • Has no online presence
  • Story doesn't add up
  • Asks for payment before showing product
  • Uses different numbers for different "businesses"
  • Can't provide any documentation

Verification by Platform

Facebook Marketplace

  • Check the seller's Facebook profile age and activity
  • Look at other items they're selling
  • Check mutual friends
  • Use Facebook's rating system if available

Jiji Kenya

  • Look at seller's other listings
  • Check if they have "Verified" badge
  • Read all reviews carefully
  • Contact through Jiji, not WhatsApp initially

Instagram Shops

  • Check account creation date
  • Look at followers/following ratio
  • Check tagged photos (not just posts)
  • Look for watermarks on product photos

WhatsApp Sellers

  • Ask for video call before payment
  • Search the number online
  • Request to see their location
  • Ask detailed questions about products
