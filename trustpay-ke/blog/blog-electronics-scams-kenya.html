Online Electronics Scams in Kenya

How to spot fake phones and laptops being sold online. Protect your money from electronics scammers in Kenya.

The demand for smartphones and laptops in Kenya continues to grow, and so do the scams. From fake iPhones to refurbished devices sold as new, fraudsters have become increasingly sophisticated. This guide helps you identify and avoid electronics scams.

Common Electronics Scams in Kenya

1. Fake Phone Scams

Scammers sell counterfeit phones that look authentic but are cheap knockoffs. They may use cloned boxes, fake IMEI numbers, and even stolen genuine phone shells.

Warning signs: Price too low, seller refuses meetup, only accepts M-PESA payments upfront.

2. Refurbished as New

Used or damaged phones are repaired, cleaned up, and sold as brand new. These phones may work initially but have hidden problems that emerge after weeks.

Warning signs: No original packaging, accessories look worn, battery health significantly degraded.

3. Stolen Phones with Fake Documents

Fraudsters sell stolen phones along with fake receipts or warranty cards to make them appear legitimate.

Warning signs: Seller can't provide original receipt, different story each time you ask, urgent sale.

4. Advance Payment Scams

Seller asks for a deposit or full payment before showing or delivering the phone. After payment, they block the buyer.

Warning signs: Requires advance payment, can't meet in person, lots of excuses.

5. Laptop Swap Scams

During a meetup, the seller swaps the genuine laptop for a fake or defective one while you're distracted.

Warning signs: Seller rushes the transaction, creates distractions, won't let you inspect thoroughly.

How to Verify Before Buying

Essential Phone Verification Steps

  • Check IMEI: Dial *#06# and verify the IMEI matches the box and settings
  • Inspect the phone: Look for signs of opening, loose screws, or mismatched parts
  • Test thoroughly: Check camera, speakers, fingerprint, face unlock, all ports
  • Check battery health: Settings > Battery on iPhone, *#*#4636#*#* on Android
  • Verify serial number: Check against manufacturer's warranty database
  • Request original receipt: A genuine seller should have it

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Price significantly lower than market value
  • Seller only communicates via WhatsApp
  • Refuses to meet in public places
  • Creates urgency: "Someone else is interested"
  • Has no online presence or reviews
  • Story changes when you ask questions
  • Won't let you test the phone properly
  • Only accepts payment before meeting

Safe Buying Practices

  • Always meet in person - Choose busy public places like shopping malls
  • Test thoroughly - Take 15-20 minutes to check everything
  • Bring a friend - Two pairs of eyes are better than one
  • Use escrow - If you can't meet, use TrustPay KE to protect your payment
  • Buy from verified sellers - Authorized dealers and established shops
  • Trust your instincts - If something feels wrong, walk away
