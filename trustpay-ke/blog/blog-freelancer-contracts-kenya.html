Freelancer Contracts: Essential Clauses Every Kenyan Freelancer Must Include

Protect your work and get paid fairly. This comprehensive guide covers essential contract clauses that every Kenyan freelancer should include in their agreements.

Every Kenyan freelancer has horror stories about clients who refused to pay, changed project requirements mid-way, or disappeared after receiving work. A proper contract protects both parties and sets clear expectations.

Why Kenyan Freelancers Need Contracts

Many Kenyan freelancers work based on verbal agreements or simple WhatsApp messages. This leaves them vulnerable. A written contract provides legal protection and professional credibility.

Essential Contract Clauses

1. Scope of Work

Define exactly what you'll deliver. Include specific deliverables, file formats, number of revisions, and timeline. Vague scopes lead to scope creep and unpaid extra work.

2. Payment Terms

Specify the total amount, payment schedule, and acceptable payment methods. Always require upfront payment or milestone payments for large projects. Never start work without any payment commitment.

3. Revisions Limit

Include the number of revisions included (typically 2-3 rounds). Specify additional revision fees to prevent clients from requesting endless changes.

4. Ownership & Copyright

Clarify when full ownership transfers to the client. Many freelancers retain rights until full payment is received. Include clauses about portfolio usage rights.

5. Timeline & Deadlines

Specify start date, milestone dates, and final delivery date. Include what happens if client delays feedback - this often causes project delays.

6. Termination Clause

Define how either party can end the contract. Specify payment for work completed if terminated early. This protects you if a client becomes unresponsive or difficult.

7. Confidentiality

Include NDA provisions if working on sensitive projects. Protect your own trade secrets and methodologies as well.

8. Dispute Resolution

Specify how disputes will be handled. Include mediation process and jurisdiction (Kenya courts).

Sample Payment Clause

Payment Structure Example

  • 50% upfront: Required before work begins
  • 25% at midpoint: Upon completion of first major milestone
  • 25% final: Upon completion and client approval

Note: For projects under KES 20,000, request 100% upfront or 50% upfront with escrow protection.

💡 Pro Tips

Always use escrow for new clients. It's not insulting - it shows professionalism. Get contracts signed before starting. Document all communications. Keep records for at least 3 years.
