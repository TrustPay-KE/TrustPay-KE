Where Your Money Is Held

Dedicated Escrow Bank Account

Client funds are securely held in a dedicated escrow bank account registered under TrustPay Services (Business No: BN-L3SD8935). This account is strictly used for holding transaction funds and is completely separate from our operational accounts.

Unlike informal escrow arrangements where money sits in personal accounts, TrustPay maintains a dedicated business account exclusively for client funds. This ensures:

Fund Segregation

Client funds are completely separate from operating expenses. Your money is never used for business costs.

Secure Storage

Funds are held in a regulated bank account with full audit trails and transparency.

Conditional Release

Funds are released ONLY when both parties confirm the transaction is complete.

No Personal Accounts

We never use personal accounts to store client funds. Everything is professionally managed.

How Escrow Protection Works

When you use TrustPay for a transaction, your money follows this secure process:

The Escrow Process:

  1. Agreement: Buyer and seller agree on terms, price, and delivery conditions
  2. Payment: Buyer deposits funds to TrustPay's dedicated escrow account via M-PESA
  3. Confirmation: We verify receipt and confirm escrow is active
  4. Holding: Funds are securely held until conditions are met
  5. Delivery: Seller delivers goods or completes service
  6. Verification: Buyer confirms receipt and satisfaction
  7. Release: Funds released to seller (minus small service fee)

Fund Release & Dispute Resolution

TrustPay ensures timely fund release based on transaction type while protecting both parties through our professional dispute resolution process.

Fund Release Timelines:

  1. Instant Services (design, writing, coding, consultations): Funds released within 30 minutes of buyer confirmation. If buyer doesn't respond within 48 hours, auto-release to seller.
  2. Digital Goods (files, accounts, gift cards, software): 6-24 hour verification window after delivery. Buyer can confirm or request dispute during this period.
  3. Physical Goods (items requiring delivery): 24 hours after delivery confirmation. Allows time for inspection before funds release to seller.
  4. Custom Agreement: Release timeline agreed upon by both buyer and seller before payment is made.

Dispute Resolution Process:

  1. Report Issue: Either party can open a dispute within the holding period
  2. Evidence Submission: Both parties submit evidence (photos, messages, delivery proof)
  3. Review: TrustPay resolution team reviews within 24 hours
  4. Decision: Based on evidence and agreed terms, funds are released to appropriate party
  5. Resolution: Full refund to buyer OR release to seller, depending on outcome
