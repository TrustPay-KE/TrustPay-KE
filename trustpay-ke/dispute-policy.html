Dispute Resolution Policy

Last Updated: February 21, 2026

TrustPay KE is committed to providing fair and efficient dispute resolution for all escrow transactions. This policy outlines how disputes are handled when they arise between buyers and sellers.

Important: By using TrustPay KE services, you agree to this Dispute Policy and the outcome of any dispute resolution process.

1. When to Raise a Dispute

You may raise a dispute when:

  • Goods are not received
  • Goods received are significantly different from description
  • Goods are damaged or defective
  • Services were not delivered as agreed
  • Suspicious or fraudulent activity
  • Other material breach of transaction terms

2. How to Raise a Dispute

1 Contact TrustPay KE

Submit a dispute through WhatsApp, email, or our support system within 14 days of the transaction.

2 Provide Details

Include:

  • Order number
  • Description of the issue
  • Evidence (photos, messages, receipts)
  • Desired resolution

3 Await Review

TrustPay KE will acknowledge your dispute within 24 hours and begin investigation.

3. Evidence Requirements

To help resolve your dispute quickly, provide:

  • For non-delivery: Delivery confirmation screenshots, communication with seller
  • For damaged goods: Photos/videos of damage, packaging photos
  • For not-as-described: Original listing, what was received, comparison
  • For non-delivery of services: Agreement/contract, work progress evidence
  • For all: M-PESA transaction confirmations, relevant messages

4. Dispute Resolution Process

Stage 1: Initial Review (1-3 days)

TrustPay KE reviews the dispute and requests additional information if needed. Both parties are notified of the claim.

Stage 2: Response Period (3-5 days)

The other party has an opportunity to respond with their evidence. Both parties may communicate through TrustPay KE.

Stage 3: Investigation (5-7 days)

TrustPay KE reviews all evidence, considers transaction terms, and evaluates the claim based on the information provided.

Stage 4: Decision

TrustPay KE issues a final decision. This may include:

  • Full refund to buyer
  • Release of funds to seller
  • Partial refund
  • Split of funds between parties

5. Possible Outcomes

For Buyers:

  • Full Refund: If dispute is upheld, entire transaction amount returned
  • Partial Refund: For minor issues or shared responsibility
  • Funds Released: If evidence supports seller

For Sellers:

  • Funds Released: If evidence supports successful delivery
  • Partial Payment: If buyer received partial value
  • No Payment: If seller cannot prove delivery

6. Time Limits

  • Dispute Window: 14 days from transaction date
  • Evidence Submission: 7 days after dispute is raised
  • Response Period: 5 days to respond to claims
  • Appeal Period: 7 days to appeal decision

Important Deadlines

  • Disputes must be raised within 14 days
  • Failure to provide evidence may result in automatic decision against you
  • Appeals must be submitted within 7 days of decision

7. Fees and Refunds

  • Dispute Fee: KES 200 (refundable if dispute upheld)
  • TrustPay KE Fee: Non-refundable unless determined by dispute outcome
  • Refunds: Original payment method (M-PESA)

8. What We Consider

When making decisions, TrustPay KE considers:

  • Communication history between parties
  • Evidence of delivery or non-delivery
  • Transaction terms agreed upon
  • Market value of items/services
  • Buyer and seller history on platform
  • Relevant M-PESA transaction records

9. Prohibited Dispute Behavior

The following may result in account termination and loss of funds:

  • False claims or fraud
  • Staged disputes (buyer and seller colluding)
  • Harassment of TrustPay KE staff
  • Providing false evidence
  • Multiple frivolous disputes

10. Appeals

If you disagree with a decision:

  • Submit appeal within 7 days
  • Provide new evidence not previously available
  • Appeal fee: KES 500 (refundable if appeal succeeds)
  • Final decision is binding

11. Final Resolution

TrustPay KE's decision is final and binding. By using our service, you agree to accept our dispute resolution outcomes.
