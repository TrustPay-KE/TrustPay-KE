Understanding Online Transaction Risks in Kenya

Kenya's digital economy has experienced remarkable growth, with M-PESA processing billions of shillings in transactions daily. However, this growth has also attracted malicious actors seeking to exploit unsuspecting users. Understanding these risks is the first step toward protecting yourself.

The most common risks include advance fee fraud, where criminals demand payments upfront with promises of goods or services that never materialise; identity theft, where fraudsters impersonate legitimate sellers; non-delivery scams, where buyers pay for items that never arrive; and payment diversion, where criminals intercept transfer details to redirect funds.

Did You Know?

The Kenya Police Service reports that online fraud accounts for approximately 15% of all reported crimes in the country, with losses averaging KES 45,000 per incident. However, with proper precautions, virtually all these scams are preventable.

How Escrow Services Protect You

Escrow services act as a trusted intermediary between buyers and sellers, holding funds securely until both parties fulfill their obligations. This neutral third-party approach eliminates the fundamental trust problem that makes online transactions risky.

The Escrow Process Explained

  1. Agreement: Buyer and seller agree on terms, including price, delivery timeline, and any conditions.
  2. Payment to Escrow: Buyer deposits the full amount plus any fees into a secure escrow account controlled by the service provider.
  3. Verification: The escrow service confirms receipt of funds and notifies both parties that the transaction is protected.
  4. Delivery: Seller delivers the goods or completes the services as agreed.
  5. Inspection: Buyer inspects and verifies the goods or services meet the agreed specifications.
  6. Release: Upon buyer confirmation, the escrow service releases funds to the seller.

Why Escrow Works

With escrow, neither party can cheat the other. The seller knows the buyer has funds available (they're already in escrow). The buyer knows the seller won't receive payment until goods are delivered satisfactorily. This mutual guarantee transforms陌生 (stranger) transactions into safe, verifiable exchanges.

M-PESA Security Best Practices

M-PESA is the backbone of Kenya's mobile money ecosystem, but its widespread adoption has made it a target for fraudsters. Follow these essential security practices:

Protecting Your M-PESA PIN

  • Never share your M-PESA PIN with anyone, including family members
  • Do not write your PIN down or store it in your phone
  • Cover the keypad when entering your PIN at agents or POS terminals
  • Change your PIN regularly and immediately if you suspect compromise
  • Never enter your PIN on a phone call or in response to an SMS

Red Flags: M-PESA Scam Indicators

Beware of calls, texts, or messages claiming to be from Safaricom asking for your PIN, offering prize money requiring M-PESA payment, threatening account suspension unless you verify details, or requesting reverse transfers for "verification."

Common Scams in Kenya

Awareness of common scam patterns is your best defence. Here are the most prevalent schemes targeting Kenyan consumers:

Jiji and Facebook Marketplace Scams

Fraudsters post attractive listings at below-market prices to lure buyers. They often claim to be travelling or unable to meet in person, insisting on prepayment through M-PESA. Once payment is received, they disappear or send counterfeit goods.

Freelance Payment Fraud

Clients may send fake payment confirmations, overpay and request refunds, or claim the work delivered is unsatisfactory to avoid payment. Always verify payment receipt through official M-PESA channels before commencing work.

WhatsApp Business Impersonation

Scammers create WhatsApp profiles mimicking legitimate businesses, complete with professional-looking catalogues. They collect payments and either send nothing or inferior products.

Seller and Buyer Verification

Before engaging in any transaction, take steps to verify the other party's identity and reputation:

Verification Checklist

  • Phone Number Verification: Call the number directly to speak with the person
  • Social Media Cross-Reference: Check if profiles are genuine and have historical activity
  • Reviews and References: Request and verify references from previous transactions
  • Business Registration: For larger transactions, verify any claimed business registration
  • Physical Address: For significant purchases, verify the claimed address exists

Making Safe Transactions

Follow these guidelines for every online transaction:

Essential Safety Practices

Use secure payment methods

Never send money directly to sellers. Use escrow or payment services that offer protection.

Verify before paying

Confirm goods exist and are as described before releasing any payment.

Document everything

Keep screenshots of listings, messages, and payment confirmations.

Meet in safe locations

For local pickups, choose busy public places during daylight hours.

Trust your instincts

If something feels wrong, walk away. Legitimate sellers won't pressure you.

What to Do When Things Go Wrong

Even with precautions, issues can arise. Here's how to handle them:

Immediate Steps

  1. Document everything: Screenshot all communications, listings, and payment receipts
  2. Attempt resolution: Contact the other party calmly to understand their position
  3. Report to platform: If the transaction occurred on a marketplace, report through their official channels
  4. Contact your bank: If payment was made fraudulently, notify your bank immediately
  5. File police report: For significant losses, file a report with the nearest police station

Escalating to Escrow Dispute Resolution

If you used an escrow service, follow their dispute resolution process. Provide evidence including communication records, proof of payment, evidence of non-delivery or misrepresentation, and any attempts at direct resolution.

Pre-Transaction Safety Checklist
