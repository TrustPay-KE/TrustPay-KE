Courier & Delivery Scams in Kenya

How fraudsters use fake deliveries to scam Kenyans. Protect yourself from parcel and courier fraud.

As online shopping grows in Kenya, so do courier and delivery scams. Fraudsters have found creative ways to trick people into paying for packages that don't exist or aren't theirs. This guide helps you identify and avoid these scams.

Common Courier Scams

1. Fake Delivery Notifications

You receive an SMS or WhatsApp message claiming a package is waiting for you. The message asks you to click a link or call a number to "confirm delivery" or "pay clearance fees."

Reality: There's no package. The goal is to steal your personal information or M-PESA details.

2. "Cash on Delivery" Scams

You order something and pay on delivery. When the "courier" arrives, they show you the package, you pay, and only later discover the package is empty, contains trash, or is completely different from what you ordered.

3. Customs/Duty Scam

You receive a call or message claiming a package is held at customs and you need to pay "duty" or "clearance fees" to release it. These fees may seem small but accumulate, and there's no package.

4. Wrong Number Scam

Someone "accidentally" sends you a package tracking number. When you inquire, you're told the package is valuable and they need your help to claim it. Eventually, they ask for money to "process" the claim.

5. Failed Delivery Scam

You receive notification of a "failed delivery" and are asked to call a number or pay a fee to reschedule. The call centers then extract personal information or payment details.

6. Fake Courier Companies

Scammers create fake courier company websites or social media pages. You ship items to a buyer through their "courier service" and never receive payment, or the buyer never receives the items.

How to Identify Fake Delivery Messages

Check These Elements

  • Sender number: Official couriers use shortcodes (e.g., G4S, DHL), not regular phone numbers
  • Links: Hover over links (on computer) to see the actual URL
  • Grammar: Official messages are well-written; scams often have errors
  • Urgency: Legitimate couriers don't pressure you to act immediately
  • Requests for payment: Genuine couriers don't ask for payment via WhatsApp
  • Verify independently: Don't use numbers in the message - look up the company yourself

Legitimate Courier Contact Information

When in doubt, contact the courier company directly using official channels:

  • Kenya Post: 0711 064 000 / customerservice@posta.co.ke
  • G4S Kenya: 020 490 0000
  • DHL Kenya: 020 695 0000
  • FedEx Kenya: 020 822 2222
  • Upset: 0709 777 000

How Sellers Can Protect Themselves

  • Use reputable couriers - Stick to well-known companies
  • Track all shipments - Use tracking numbers and share with buyers
  • Require ID for collection - Buyer should present ID when picking up
  • Use escrow for high-value items - Payment held until delivery confirmed
  • Take photos of packaged items - Proof of what was shipped
  • Get signatures on delivery - Proof of receipt

How Buyers Can Protect Themselves

  • Track your orders - Use tracking numbers from official sources
  • Don't click suspicious links - Go directly to courier website instead
  • Verify unexpected packages - Don't accept packages you didn't order
  • Inspect before paying COD - Open and check before handing over money
  • Never share OTPs - No courier will ask for your verification code
  • Use escrow for online purchases - Pay through TrustPay KE, not direct M-PESA

What to Do If Scammed

  • Contact Safaricom - Report fraudulent M-PESA transactions
  • File police report - Cybercrimes unit can investigate
  • Report to courier - Help them shut down fake accounts
  • Warn others - Share on social media to prevent more victims
  • Contact your bank - If you shared banking details

Remember

Real couriers will never:

  • Ask for your M-PESA PIN
  • Request payment via WhatsApp
  • Send you links via SMS
  • Demand "clearance fees" for personal packages
  • Ask for your OTP or verification code
