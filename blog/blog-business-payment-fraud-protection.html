Protecting Your Business from Payment Fraud - Kenya Guide

As Kenyan businesses increasingly move transactions online, payment fraud has become a significant threat. Based on my experience helping businesses secure their transactions, here's what every SME owner needs to know.

Common Business Payment Frauds in Kenya

1. Fake Payment Proofs

Scammers send fake M-Pesa confirmation screenshots, claiming they've paid. Always verify payments through your actual M-Pesa account or bank statement.

2. Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent emails or SMS messages pretending to be from banks or M-Pesa, asking for login credentials. Never click links in unexpected messages.

3. Account Takeover

Criminals gain access to business accounts through stolen credentials or SIM swaps, then drain funds.

4. Fake Invoices

Scammers send fake invoices for goods or services, hoping your accounts payable will pay without verification.

Critical: Verify All Large Payments

For any payment over KES 10,000, always confirm directly with the sender through a known contact number before processing goods or services.

Essential Protection Measures

For M-Pesa Businesses

  • Use a dedicated business phone number for M-Pesa
  • Enable all available security features
  • Separate business and personal M-Pesa accounts
  • Train all staff on fraud recognition
  • Implement approval workflows for large transactions

For Bank Transactions

  • Use dual authentication for all payments
  • Regularly review bank statements
  • Set up transaction alerts
  • Limit who can initiate payments

Use Escrow for High-Value Sales

For sales over KES 5,000 to new customers, always use escrow services like TrustPay KE. We hold the funds until the buyer confirms receipt, eliminating the risk of fake payments.

Building a Fraud-Resistant Culture

The best defense is a team that understands fraud risks. Train your staff to:

  • Verify all payment claims independently
  • Question unusual requests, especially urgency
  • Report suspicious activity immediately
  • Follow verification procedures consistently

Conclusion

Payment fraud is a real threat, but with proper procedures and vigilance, your business can protect itself. Take time to review your current processes and implement these recommendations. Prevention is always cheaper than recovery.
