Escrow vs Cash on Delivery in Kenya: Which Payment Method is Safer?

Cash on Delivery (COD) and escrow are two popular payment methods for online purchases in Kenya. But which one truly protects you from scams? This comprehensive guide breaks down the pros and cons of each method.

When shopping online in Kenya, choosing the right payment method can mean the difference between a successful purchase and a costly scam. Two popular options are Cash on Delivery (COD) and escrow services. But which one is truly safer for Kenyan consumers?

Understanding Cash on Delivery (COD)

Cash on Delivery is exactly what it sounds like - you pay cash when you receive the product. This payment method has been popular in Kenya because it allows buyers to inspect products before paying.

Understanding Escrow

With escrow, your money is held by a trusted third party (TrustPay KE) until you confirm receipt and satisfaction with the product. Only then is the money released to the seller.

When COD Makes Sense

  • Small cash purchases: Under KES 1,000, COD is generally safe
  • Local face-to-face: Meeting seller in person to inspect and pay
  • Trusted sellers: You personally know the seller
  • Simple items: Products you can easily evaluate upon delivery

When Escrow is Essential

  • Large purchases: Over KES 5,000 - always use escrow
  • Unknown sellers: First-time purchases from new sellers
  • Online-only transactions: No in-person meeting
  • Service payments: Freelance work, consultations
  • High-value items: Electronics, vehicles, property
  • Custom orders: Items made to specification

⚠️ COD Risks You Should Know

Despite seeming safe, COD has significant vulnerabilities. Some sellers demand "processing fees" before delivery. Others deliver counterfeit or damaged goods expecting you to refuse, then charge you for "return shipping." Additionally, with COD there's no documentation of what was agreed upon - it's your word against theirs if something goes wrong.

💡 The Verdict

For purchases under KES 2,000 where you can meet face-to-face and inspect the item, COD is acceptable. For anything larger, or any transaction where you won't physically meet the seller, escrow is the only safe choice.
