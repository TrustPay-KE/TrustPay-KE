Common Payment Scams Targeting Kenyan SMEs

Small and medium enterprises (SMEs) in Kenya are increasingly targeted by sophisticated scammers. Understanding these scams is your first line of defense.

Top Scams Affecting Kenyan SMEs

1. Fake Client Scams

Scammers pose as new clients, placing large orders and paying in advance. Then they "accidentally" overpay and ask for a refund - the original payment bounces.

2. WhatsApp Business Scams

Fraudsters create fake WhatsApp business accounts impersonating well-known companies, then approach SMEs for "deals."

3. Tax Refund Scams

Scammers call SMEs claiming to be from KRA, offering tax refunds and requesting bank details to "process" the refund.

4. Fake Supplier Scams

Scammers set up fake supplier websites or profiles, take payment for goods that never arrive, and disappear.

5. Business Email Compromise (BEC)

Hackers compromise business email accounts and request payment details changes from suppliers.

Red Flags to Watch

  • New client wants to pay significantly more than quoted
  • Urgency or pressure to act quickly
  • Requests to change payment details
  • Unverifiable business information
  • Poorly written communications

Protection Strategies

  • Verify all new clients before large transactions
  • Use escrow for unfamiliar customers
  • Establish payment verification procedures
  • Train staff on scam recognition
  • Never change payment details without direct verification

Safe Payment Practices

Always confirm payment receipts independently through your banking or M-Pesa channels. Never rely solely on confirmation screenshots or messages.
