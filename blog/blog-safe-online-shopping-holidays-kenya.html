Safe Online Shopping During Kenyan Holidays - Complete Guide

Holiday seasons see a spike in online shopping - and unfortunately, a spike in scams too. Here's how to protect yourself and your family while enjoying the festive shopping experience.

Last Christmas, my cousin James wanted to buy a new laptop for his daughter. He found what looked like a great deal on Instagram - 40% off a MacBook. The seller had hundreds of followers and glowing reviews. By the time he realized it was a scam, KES 85,000 was gone.

Stories like James's become alarmingly common during Kenyan holiday seasons. Scammers know that everyone is shopping - families are buying gifts, people are treating themselves, and there's a general atmosphere of generosity and excitement. That excitement makes us vulnerable.

In this guide, I'll share practical strategies to help you shop safely during Kenya's peak shopping seasons.

Why Holidays Attract Scammers

Understanding why holiday seasons are scammer paradise helps you stay vigilant:

  • Increased shopping volume: More people buying online means more potential victims
  • Urgency culture: "Limited time offers" and "only a few left" create pressure
  • Distraction: People are busy with celebrations and less cautious
  • Generosity: Holiday spirit makes us more trusting
  • New online shoppers: First-time buyers unfamiliar with warning signs

Black Friday Warning

Black Friday has become one of the biggest shopping events in Kenya, but it's also when scam activity peaks. In 2025, consumer protection agencies reported a 300% increase in online shopping complaints during Black Friday week compared to normal periods.

10 Holiday Shopping Safety Rules

1. Research Before You Buy

Even for "amazing deals" you're excited about, take 10 minutes to verify the seller. Search their name plus "scam" or "review." Check if they have a physical business location.

2. Use Secure Payment Methods

Never send money directly via M-PESA to unknown sellers, especially for high-value items. Use escrow services, credit cards when available, or cash on delivery if possible.

3. Verify URLs Carefully

Scammers create fake websites that look almost identical to real ones. Double-check the URL spelling. For example, "amaz0n.com" instead of "amazon.com" - these tricks work during rushed holiday shopping.

4. Don't Fall for Deep Discounts

If a price seems too good to be true, it probably is. Scammers use irresistible discounts to lure victims. A KES 150,000 iPhone for KES 45,000 is not a deal - it's a trap.

5. Check Website Security

Before entering payment details, ensure the website has "https://" at the beginning and a padlock icon. But remember - scammers can also use HTTPS, so this alone isn't enough.

6. Avoid Public WiFi for Shopping

Never shop using public WiFi at malls, cafes, or hotels. These networks are often unsecured and can be intercepted by scammers.

7. Keep Transaction Records

Screenshot your order confirmation, save email receipts, and keep communication records. This evidence is crucial if something goes wrong.

8. Be Wary of Social Media Ads

Social media advertising is largely unregulated. Just because you saw an ad doesn't mean the seller is legitimate. Many scam ads run for just a few hours during peak season.

9. Set Spending Limits

Plan your holiday budget and stick to it. Scammers prey on overspending - if a deal pushes you beyond your budget, that's a warning sign.

10. Trust Your Instincts

If something feels wrong about a seller or transaction, walk away. There will always be other deals, but the money you lose to a scammer can't be recovered.

Safe Holiday Shopping Checklist

  • Verified the seller through multiple sources
  • Using secure payment method (escrow or card)
  • Checked URL for authenticity
  • Read return and refund policies
  • Saved order confirmation
  • Shopping within budget

What To Do If Scammed During the Holidays

Despite your best efforts, sometimes the worst happens. Here's your action plan:

  1. Act immediately: Contact Safaricom for reversal if money was sent recently
  2. Gather evidence: Screenshot the listing, save messages, note transaction details
  3. Report to platforms: Report the seller to Facebook, Instagram, Jiji, etc.
  4. File police report: The Economic Crimes Unit can pursue scammers
  5. Warn others: Share your experience to help others avoid the same scam

Safe Shopping Alternatives

Rather than risk dealing with unknown sellers, consider these safer options:

  • Authorized retailers: Buy electronics and appliances from brand-authorized stores
  • Established e-commerce: Use platforms with buyer protection
  • Escrow services: For peer-to-peer transactions, always use escrow
  • Cash on delivery: When available, this protects your money until you receive goods

Remember

The holidays are a time for celebration, not regret. A few extra minutes of verification can save you thousands of shillings and the heartbreak of being scammed. Share these tips with your family - especially younger and older relatives who may be more vulnerable to online scams.
