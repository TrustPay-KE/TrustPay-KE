How to Start Escrow Transaction in Kenya

Complete step-by-step guide to protecting your online purchases with TrustPay KE escrow service. Safe, secure, and simple.

Online transactions in Kenya have grown exponentially, but so have scams. Whether you're buying a phone on Jiji, hiring a freelancer, or purchasing goods from an Instagram shop, the risk of losing your money to a scammer is real. This is where escrow comes in—a powerful protection mechanism that ensures both buyers and sellers are protected throughout the transaction.

What is an Escrow Transaction?

Escrow is a financial arrangement where a third party (TrustPay KE) holds the buyer's payment until the transaction is completed satisfactorily. The money is only released to the seller once the buyer confirms receipt of the goods or services as described. This eliminates the risk of fraud for both parties.

Why Use Escrow in Kenya?

With M-PESA being the dominant payment method, many Kenyans fall victim to scams where buyers refuse to pay after receiving goods or sellers disappear after receiving payment. Escrow solves this problem by creating a trusted middleman.

Step-by-Step Guide to Starting an Escrow Transaction

1 Visit TrustPay KE and Start Your Order

Go to trustpay.co.ke and click "Start Order." You'll need to choose whether you're the buyer or seller initiating the transaction. The buyer typically initiates the escrow process to deposit funds.

2 Enter Transaction Details

Fill in the required information:

  • Your phone number (M-PESA registered)
  • Other party's phone number
  • Transaction amount - the total price of goods or services
  • Description - what you're buying/selling

3 Review and Confirm Fees

TrustPay KE charges a small fee for the escrow service:

  • Transactions under KES 200: Free
  • KES 200 - KES 5,000: 0.7%
  • KES 5,001 - KES 50,000: 0.5%
  • KES 50,001+: 0.3% (maximum KES 2,500)

Review the total amount (item price + fee) before proceeding.

4 Make Payment via M-PESA

After confirming, you'll receive M-PESA payment instructions:

  • Go to M-PESA on your phone
  • Select Pay Bill
  • Enter Business Number: 542542
  • Enter Account Number: Your unique order number
  • Enter Amount: Total (price + fee)

5 Wait for Confirmation

Once you send the M-PESA, TrustPay KE will verify the payment and confirm the funds are held in escrow. You'll receive confirmation via SMS and WhatsApp.

6 Seller Delivers Goods/Services

The seller now proceeds to deliver the goods or complete the services. It's important that delivery is confirmed before the buyer releases the funds.

7 Buyer Confirms and Funds Released

Once the buyer receives the goods/services and is satisfied:

  • Buyer confirms receipt via the TrustPay platform
  • TrustPay releases funds to the seller's M-PESA
  • Transaction complete!

What Happens If There's a Dispute?

If the buyer claims they didn't receive the goods or the goods are not as described, TrustPay KE will mediate the dispute. Both parties can provide evidence, and TrustPay will make a fair decision based on the facts provided.

Common Use Cases for Escrow in Kenya

  • Online Marketplaces: Buying phones, laptops, electronics on Jiji, Facebook Marketplace
  • Freelance Work: Web developers, graphic designers, writers getting paid by clients
  • Car Purchases: High-value items where trust is crucial
  • Instagram/TikTok Shops: Buying fashion, beauty products from online sellers
  • Property Deposits: Renting apartments, paying deposits

Tips for a Smooth Escrow Transaction

  • Communicate clearly with the other party about expectations
  • Keep records of all communications
  • Take photos of goods before shipping
  • Use the platform for all communication - don't move to WhatsApp until necessary
  • Act quickly - confirm receipt promptly once satisfied
