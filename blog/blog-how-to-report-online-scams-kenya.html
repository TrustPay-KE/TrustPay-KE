How to Report Online Scams in Kenya - Complete Guide

If you've been scammed, knowing where and how to report can help you recover your money and prevent others from becoming victims.

After years of helping scam victims, I've learned that reporting is crucial - yet many Kenyans don't know where to turn when they've been defrauded. Some give up because they think nothing can be done. Others don't report because they're embarrassed.

This guide will walk you through every option available in Kenya for reporting online scams. While recovery isn't guaranteed, reporting is essential for building cases against scammers and protecting others.

Why Reporting Matters

Before we dive into the how, let's talk about why reporting is so important:

  • Recovery chances: The more people report, the higher chance authorities can identify patterns and catch scammers
  • Prevent others: Your report could save someone else from the same scam
  • Build cases: Police need multiple reports to build strong cases
  • Platform action: Reports lead to scam accounts being shut down

Act Fast - Time is Critical

If you've sent money recently, contact Safaricom IMMEDIATELY for a possible reversal. The chances of recovery drop dramatically after 24 hours.

Where to Report Online Scams in Kenya

1. Safaricom M-Pesa Reversal (Immediate Action)

For M-Pesa transactions, this is your first step:

  • Call 234 (toll-free) from your Safaricom line
  • Or visit the nearest Safaricom Shop or Agent
  • Provide transaction details: date, time, amount, recipient number
  • Request a reversal citing fraudulent transaction

2. Police Reports

The following police units handle online fraud:

Economic Crimes Unit (ECU)

Located at the DCI Headquarters, Nairobi

Specializes in financial crimes and online fraud

Local Police Station

File report at your nearest station

Get a OB Number for your case

Online Reporting

Use the DCI e-reporting portal

Available at dci.go.ke

3. Platform Reports

Report the scammer to where you encountered them:

  • Facebook: Use the "Report" option on the profile or listing
  • Instagram: Report the account via Settings > Report
  • Jiji: Use the "Report" button on listings
  • WhatsApp: Report through the chat info menu

4. Communication Authority of Kenya (CA)

For scams involving telecommunications:

  • Email: complaints@ca.go.ke
  • Hotline: 0800221000
  • They can investigate fraudulent SIM cards and numbers

5. Consumer Protection

The Competition Authority of Kenya handles consumer fraud:

  • Website: cakenya.com
  • Email: info@cakenya.com
  • Report unfair business practices and scams

What Information to Include

  • Your name and contact details
  • Date and time of transaction
  • Amount lost
  • Scammer's phone number(s)
  • Screenshots of conversations
  • Any other evidence you have
  • How you found the seller (platform, ad, etc.)

What Happens After You Report

Understanding the process helps manage expectations:

  1. Police investigation: They may contact you for more details
  2. Platform action: Scammer's account gets banned
  3. Awareness: Your report helps warn others
  4. Court case (rare): If scammers are caught and charged

Prevention is Better Than Cure

While knowing how to report is important, preventing scams in the first place is always better. Always verify sellers, use escrow for high-value purchases, and trust your instincts when something feels off about a deal.
